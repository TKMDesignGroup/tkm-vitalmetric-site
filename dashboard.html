How meals are structured

The goal is simple, repeatable meals that fit your week instead of fighting it:

  • Breakfast anchors: eggs, oats, yogurt, or toast-based combos
  • Lunches built from bowls, wraps, or leftovers
  • Dinners drawn from a small set of “go-to” mains
  • Snacks planned in (applesauce, crackers, light desserts)

How sodium gets reduced

The demo plan leans on swaps, not strict bans:

  • Fewer canned soups and ultra-salty frozen meals
  • More herbs, spice mixes, citrus, and vinegar instead of salt
  • Higher-sodium meals paired with higher-activity days
  • Room to adjust based on how your body responds

Movement stack, baked in

Light, realistic movement is slotted into your days by design:

  • Daily Radio Taiso or similar warmup block
  • Tai Chi sessions in calmer parts of the day
  • Step goal centered around ~2,500 steps as a baseline
  • Easy to scale up on days when you feel good

Budget and real-life constraints

The prototype assumes a weekly grocery budget like $75 and makes choices around that:

  • Start with pantry and freezer, then fill the gaps
  • Use leftovers to stretch proteins and sides
  • Reserve “flex” meals for store sales and specials
  • Keep a printable list so grocery trips stay focused
