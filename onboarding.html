Start your weekly plan (13-step flow)

This is the full, detailed version of the TKM VitalMetric Wellness onboarding wizard. In the live system, your answers here would drive your Weekly Snapshot, weekly meal plan, grocery list, movement stack, and printable report.

Prototype only — your answers are not stored. This walkthrough is to show the structure and depth of the real system.

Step 1 of 13

Step 1 – Basic profile & goals

Tell us a bit about you so we can keep your weekly plan realistic and focused on what matters most right now.

Step 2 – Plan settings: dates, calories, budget & sodium

Set the basics for this weekly plan so your meals, snacks, and grocery list match your real-life limits.

Step 3 – Cooking comfort & kitchen setup

Help us understand what you’re working with in your kitchen and schedule so recipes match your real life.

Step 4 – Food preferences (like / neutral / dislike)

For each category, tell us how you feel about each item. In the real app, these choices would influence recipe selection and meal planning.

4.1 Proteins

For each protein, choose Like, Neutral, or Dislike.

4.2 Dairy & alternatives

4.3 Grains, starches & carbs

4.4 Fruits

4.5 Vegetables

4.6 Snacks & treats

4.7 Frozen & convenience meals

4.8 Condiments & sauces

4.9 Drinks

Step 5 – Avoid / medical restrictions

Tell us if there are any foods you must avoid for medical, allergy, or personal reasons.

Step 6 – Pantry

A big part of the plan uses the food you already have. This helps cut grocery costs and reduces waste.

Step 7 – Where you shop & how you budget

This helps the plan lean into the stores and habits you already use, instead of pretending you shop at fancy places you never visit.

Step 8 – Sodium & nutrition focus

This step controls how aggressively the plan tries to lower sodium, and what other nutrition goals it should keep in mind.

Step 9 – Movement stack & exercise comfort

This doesn’t have to be fancy. We just want realistic movement that helps your health and works with your body.

Step 10 – Daily schedule & meal timing

This helps the plan land meals and snacks in the windows that actually work for you.

Step 11 – Leftovers, batch cooking & repetition

Some people love leftovers, some people hate them. We’ll match your style.

Step 12 – Guidance & plan style

Last few style questions about how the plan should look and feel for you.

Step 13 – Summary & confirmation

In the live system, this step would show a smart summary of your answers and a preview of your upcoming weekly plan.

Prototype note: This is a demo version of the wizard. In a future version, this area would summarize:

  • Your sodium focus and nutrition priorities
  • Your favorite foods and top “never include” items
  • Your pantry, stores, budget, and movement stack
  • How structured vs. flexible your plan will feel
